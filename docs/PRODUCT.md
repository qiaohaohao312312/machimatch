# Machimatch — Product Manual

> *The first step of finding a home is the street.*

---

## 1. What We Believe

Most people search for a home by browsing apartment listings.  
We think that's the wrong starting point.

Before the apartment, there is the street.  
Before the street, there is a feeling—the pace of the morning, the sound of the neighborhood, the kind of strangers you pass.

**Machimatch helps people find the neighborhood in the world that truly fits their lifestyle—before they ever look at a floor plan.**

We believe:
- A new life should begin with a lifestyle, not a listing.
- Anywhere in the world, there exists a neighborhood you don't know yet that fits you better than where you are now.
- The best neighborhoods are felt, not filtered.

---

## 2. The Problem We Solve

Moving to a new city—or a new country—is overwhelming. People default to:
- Price filters on Airbnb or real estate apps
- Reddit threads asking "where should I live in Tokyo?"
- Vibes from Instagram photos that don't reflect real daily life

None of these tools start from the person. They start from inventory.

Machimatch flips this. We start from **who you are and how you live**, then find the place that fits.

---

## 3. Core Product Flow

### Step 1 — Where in the world?
User picks a city, a region, or says "Anywhere."  
The openness of "Anywhere" is intentional—it invites curiosity, not just logistics.

### Step 2 — Lifestyle Quiz (6–7 questions)
Not a boring form. Each question is a moment of self-reflection.

| Question | Format |
|---|---|
| What window would you like to wake up to? | Image swipe (visual, emotional) |
| What would you like within a 5-minute walk? | Multi-select cards |
| Quiet neighborhood or lively one? | Slider |
| How important is quick access to city center? | Priority scale 1–5 |
| Other things you'd like us to know... | Free text with floating examples |

The free text field is the heart of the quiz. It's where the human leaks in—*"I have a cat," "my girlfriend visits sometimes," "I need an ice cream shop nearby."* This input feeds directly into the AI.

### Step 3 — Your Top 5 Neighborhoods

Results are not a list. They are **stories**.

Each neighborhood result contains:
- **"Your Day Here"** — An AI-generated first-person narrative of what one day in this neighborhood feels like for *you specifically*, based on your quiz answers. This is the emotional centerpiece.
- A neighborhood intro (3–4 lines, human-written tone)
- A simple map (orientation only, not the focus)
- Key tags (walkable, indie cafés, quiet mornings, expat-friendly...)

Results are swipeable cards. Five neighborhoods, each with their own story.

> **Navigation note:** The quiz flows as a single continuous scroll (top to bottom), not a left-right paginated flow. No progress dots. Each question appears as the user scrolls down, creating a feeling of gradual self-discovery rather than a stepped form.

## 4. The Soul of the Product

**Machi (街) = Street / Town in Japanese**  
The name grounds the product in a specific philosophy: the street is the unit of life, not the apartment.

**Human over algorithmic**  
Every touchpoint should feel like it was made by a person who has lived in many places and genuinely wants to help you find yours. Not a database. Not a filter engine.

**Curiosity over optimization**  
We don't help people find the *cheapest* or *most convenient* neighborhood. We help them find the *most them* neighborhood. Sometimes that surprises them.

**The world is bigger than you think**  
The "Anywhere" option is a core product philosophy. We want users to discover places they never considered—a neighborhood in Lisbon, a pocket of Osaka, a corner of Buenos Aires—that fits them perfectly.

---

## 5. What Machimatch Is Not

- ❌ Not a real estate platform (no listings, no prices)
- ❌ Not a travel app (not about tourism)
- ❌ Not a relocation service (no logistics)
- ✅ The emotional and lifestyle layer that comes *before* all of the above

---

## 6. Target Users (v1)

**Primary:** Digital nomads, remote workers, people planning a move abroad  
**Secondary:** People relocating within their own country who want a lifestyle-first approach  
**Aspirational:** Anyone who has ever wondered "where in the world would I actually thrive?"
